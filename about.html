How I became a designer...

When I was eight years old I received my first art kit. The kind that folds up
with a handle so you can take your art supplies anywhere. I would take the
kit, walk behind our house and into the 40 acre wooded property my Dad
owned in rural Paint Lick, Kentucky (Yes, in case you were wondering Paint
Lick is an actual town). I would find a nice spot and sit for hours while I made
art. Looking back on those moments I canâ€™t decide what part about that
memory I love the most; me quietly making art in the woods, or the feeling of
freedom to go anywhere I wanted and do something I truly loved. I think the
two are intricately connected. From the time I was eight I knew one thing for
sure. I loved to make and build stuff. I went to college and graduate school
where I concentrated in art and design. My professional path led me to
teach art, as well as, become an instructional designer. I have an enthusiasm
for beautifully crafted designs. In addition to my love for design, I also love
learning about other people and building relationships.
